Croatian Cheese Strudel

This is my one of my favorite recipes from my grandma.

List of Ingredients:

  • 3 eggs
  • 32 oz cottage cheese
  • 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • Phyllo dough (room temperature)
  • 3-4 tbs butter
  • Small container heavy whipping cream

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees
  2. Put the raisins to soak in some water
  3. Zest and juice the lemon.
  4. Whisk eggs, cottage cheese, lemon zest, and lemon juice together.
  5. Melt the butter and spread in container to bake in.
  6. Place 3 sheets of phyllo in container, one at a time, putting melted butter in between each sheet.
  7. Take half the filling mix and place in baking container. Sprinkle half raisins on top.
  8. Place 3 more sheets of phyllo, filling, raisins, top with 3 sheets phyllo.
  9. Pour whipping cream on the strudel.
  10. Bake for 45-50 minutes
  11. Enjoy!

    12. Chocolate Chip Cookies

    Cookies are always a comforting food

    Recipe From Cooking Classy

    List of Ingredients:

    • 2 Tbs salted butter
    • 3 Tbsp light brown sugar
    • 1 pinch salt
    • 1 Tbsp whisked egg
    • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
    • 1/3 cup all purpose flour
    • 1/8 tsp baking soda
    • 4 Tbsp chocolate chips

    Steps

    1. Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C). Place butter in a microwave safe mixing bowl. Heat in microwave until nearly melted (you want it almost all the way melted, remove from microwave just before completely melted and stir to fully melt. You just don't want it too hot or it will cook the egg and melt the chocolate chips when stirring).
    2. To melted butter, add brown sugar, salt, 1 tbsp egg, the vanilla, flour and baking soda. Stir with a spoon until well combined, about 20 - 30 seconds.
    3. Mix in 3 tablespoons of the chocolate chips
    4. Shape dough into a 3 1/2-inch disk on a Silpat or parchment paper lined baking sheet, gently press remaining 1 Tbsp chocolate chips randomly over top and sides of cookie.
    5. Bake in preheated oven 12 - 14 minutes, until cookie is golden. Serve warm or allow to cool on a wire rack then store in an airtight container.
    6. Enjoy!

    Lemon Curd

    Who doesn't like lemon curd

    Recipe From Sally's Baking Addiction

    List of Ingredients:

    • 4 large egg yolks
    • 2/3 cup granulated sugar
    • 1 Tbsp lemon zest
    • 1/3 cup fresh lemon juice
    • 1/8 teaspoon salt
    • 6 Tbsp unsalted butter softened

    Steps

    1. Fill the bottom pot of your double boiler with 1-2 inches of water. (Or use the DIY double boiler method listed in the notes.) Place on high heat. Once the water begins to boil, reduce to low heat to keep the water at a simmer.
    2. Place egg yolks, granulated sugar, lemon zest, lemon juice, and salt into the top pot of your double boiler. Using a silicone whisk, whisk until completely blended, then continue to whisk as the curd cooks. Constant whisking prevents the egg yolks from curdling. Whisk and cook until the mixture becomes thick, resembling the texture of hollandaise sauce, about 10 minutes. If curd isn’t thickening, turn up the heat and constantly whisk.
    3. Remove pan from heat. Cut the butter into 6 separate pieces, then whisk into the curd. The butter will melt from the heat of the curd. Pour curd into a jar or bowl and place a piece of plastic wrap directly on top so it is touching the top of the curd. (This prevents a skin from forming on top.) The curd will continue to thicken as it cools. Once cool, the plastic wrap can be removed.
    4. Refrigerate the curd for up to about 10 days.
    5. Enjoy!
